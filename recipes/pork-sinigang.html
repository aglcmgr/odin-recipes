Pork Sinigang

⏱️ 30 mins ⭐ Easy 👥 4 servings

This Pork Sinigang is a traditional Filipino dish that combines tender pork with a tangy and savory broth. Perfect for a hearty meal, this recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings for authentic Filipino flavors.

Image credits to Vanjo Merano ~ Panlasang Pinoy

It is a beloved Filipino comfort dish featuring tender pork simmered in a tangy tamarind broth with fresh vegetables like okra, radish, and string beans. The combination of tamarind and fish sauce creates a deeply savory and sour flavor that's both warming and satisfying. This hearty stew is quick to prepare, affordable, and perfect for family meals served over steaming rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. Pork belly cubed
  • 66 g. Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix with Gabi
  • 2 pieces talong sliced
  • 8 pieces okra
  • 18 pieces string beans cut in 2-inch pieces
  • 5 ounces daikon radish (labanos) sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong
  • 1 piece onion wedged
  • 2 pieces tomato wedged
  • 6 pieces shishito pepper
  • 8 cups water
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil

Equipments Needed

  1. ✓ Large pot (6-8 quart)
  2. ✓ Cutting board
  3. ✓ Sharp knife
  4. ✓ Wooden spoon or ladle
  5. ✓ Measuring cups & spoons
  6. ✓ Can opener (for sinigang mix)

Image credits to Vanjo Merano ~ Panlasang Pinoy

Cooking Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a cooking pot. Saute onion until layers separate. Add half of the tomato. Saute for 2 minutes.
  2. Add pork belly. Continue sauteeing until the pork browns while adding around 1 tablespoon fish sauce.
  3. Pour water. Cover the pot and let the liquid boil.
  4. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc with Gabi. Cover and adjust heat between low to medium. Cook for 30 minutes or until the pork gets tender.
  5. Add labanos. Cover and continue cooking for 30 minutes.
  6. Add eggplant, string beans, and okra. Cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Add kangkong stalks and remaining tomato. Cook for 3 minutes.
  8. Season with fish sauce (as needed) and ground black pepper.
