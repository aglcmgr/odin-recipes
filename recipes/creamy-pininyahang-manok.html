Creamy Pininyahang Manok

⏱️ 20 mins ⭐ Easy 👥 6 servings

This Creamy Pininyahang Manok is a rich and flavorful chicken soup that is perfect for a comforting meal. This traditional Filipino dish combines the tenderness of chicken with the creaminess of coconut milk, creating a delicious and satisfying soup.

It is a traditional Filipino chicken soup that is rich and flavorful. It is made with tender chicken, aromatic spices, and creamy milk, creating a delicious and satisfying dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken cut into serving pieces
  • 20 ounces pineapple chunks canned
  • 2 medium potatoes diced
  • 2 medium carrots cut into wedges
  • 1 red bell pepper sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper sliced
  • 200 ml Carnation Everyday Creamer
  • 2 pieces tomatoes chopped
  • 1 piece onion thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons garlic minced
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 cup cooking oil for frying and sautéing

Equipments Needed

  1. ✓ Large pan or skillet (for frying)
  2. ✓ Large pot with lid (for simmering)
  3. ✓ Cutting board
  4. ✓ Sharp knife
  5. ✓ Measuring cups and spoons
  6. ✓ Wooden spoon or spatula (for stirring)
  7. ✓ Tongs or long fork
  8. ✓ Oil skimmer or wide spoon (optional, for removing excess oil)
  9. ✓ Serving plate or bowls

Image credits to Panlasang Pinoy ~ Vanjo Merano

Cooking Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Fry the potatoes and carrots until they start to brown. Remove and set aside.
  2. Reduce the oil to 3 tablespoons. Sauté garlic, onion, and tomato until softened.
  3. Add the chicken. Cook until it turns light brown.
  4. Pour in the pineapple juice from the canned pineapple along with the pineapple chunks. Cover and simmer for 30 minutes. Add water if needed.
  5. Add the bell peppers, fried potatoes, and carrots. Simmer for 3 minutes.
  6. Season with fish sauce and ground black pepper. Pour in the Carnation Everyday Creamer. Stir and simmer for 2 minutes.
  7. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with rice.
